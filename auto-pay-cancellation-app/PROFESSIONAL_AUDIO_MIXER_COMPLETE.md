## 📊 TRACK TYPES

### Voiceover
- Main dialogue/narration
- Typically 3-6dB louder than music
- Should be clear and intelligible

### Music
- Background music
- Typically 6-9dB below voiceover
- Supports the mood

### Sound Effect
- Impact sounds, transitions, ambience
- Typically 3-6dB below music
- Draws attention to specific moments

### Ambience
- Background environment sounds
- Subtle, supporting layer
- Adds realism and space

## 💡 PROFESSIONAL MIXING TIPS

### Tip 1: Reference Monitoring
- Mix at moderate levels (not too loud)
- Take breaks to prevent ear fatigue
- A/B compare with reference tracks

### Tip 2: Frequency Balance
- Voiceover: Boost mids (1-3kHz)
- Bass: Keep controlled, not muddy
- Treble: Add for clarity, not harshness

### Tip 3: Dynamic Range
- Use compression for consistency
- Preserve natural dynamics
- Don't over-compress

### Tip 4: Spatial Imaging
- Use pan for stereo width
- Ambience should be wider than dialogue
- Keep voiceover center

### Tip 5: Automation
- Automate volume for natural flow
- Dip music under dialogue
- Build dynamics over time

### Tip 6: Headroom
- Leave -3dB to -6dB headroom
- Prevents clipping
- Allows for mastering

## 🎓 BUILT-IN TUTORIALS

### How to Mix Voiceover
1. Set voiceover volume to -6dB below other elements
2. Apply gentle compression (3:1 ratio)
3. Add slight EQ boost to mids (+2dB around 1-2kHz)
4. Apply light reverb (20-30ms) for space
5. Fade in/out for smooth transitions

### How to Mix Background Music
1. Set music volume 6-9dB below voiceover
2. Apply compression for consistency
3. Use EQ to reduce low-end muddiness
4. Add subtle reverb for depth
5. Use automation to duck volume under dialogue

### How to Mix Sound Effects
1. Set SFX volume 3-6dB below music
2. Pan effects for stereo width
3. Apply short reverb (10-20ms)
4. Use compression to control peaks
5. Add fades to blend with music

### Basic Mastering
1. Apply master compression (4:1 ratio, -20dB threshold)
2. Use master EQ to balance frequency spectrum
3. Check levels at -3dB to -6dB on master
4. A/B test with reference material
5. Export at 24-bit or higher
