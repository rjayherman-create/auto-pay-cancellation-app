## 🎯 PRO TIPS

### Voiceover Mixing
- **Compression**: 4:1 ratio, -20dB threshold, 5ms attack
- **EQ**: Reduce 100-200Hz (boominess), boost 2-4kHz (clarity)
- **Gain**: -3dB to prevent clipping
- **Pan**: Center (0.0) for mono dialogue

### Background Music
- **Volume**: 0.5x to 0.7x under dialogue
- **EQ**: Reduce 2-4kHz to not compete with voice

- **Pan**: Place in stereo field for immersion

- **Compression**: 2:1 ratio, -12dB threshold (gentle glue)
- **Gain**: Set to prevent digital clipping
- **Visual**: Watch peak meter - stay below 0dB

## 🔊 METERING & LEVELS

### Optimal Levels
- **Peaks**: -6dB to -3dB
- **Average**: -12dB to -9dB
- **Headroom**: 3dB above peaks
- **Master**: Leave 6dB headroom

### Clipping Prevention
- Red zone on meters = clipping
- Keep peaks in yellow zone
- Use gain reduction, not volume reduction

### Loudness Standards
- **YouTube**: -14LUFS (average loudness)
- **Broadcast**: -23LUFS to -24LUFS
- **Streaming**: -14LUFS
- **CD**: -9LUFS

## 🔧 TROUBLESHOOTING

**Clipping/Distortion**
- Lower individual track gains
- Reduce master volume
- Check peak meters

**Muddy/Bassy Mix**
- Reduce low shelf on problem tracks
- Increase High Shelf slightly
- Check kick/bass levels

**Thin/Harsh Mix**
- Boost Low Shelf slightly
- Reduce High Shelf
- Add compression for cohesion

**Unbalanced Levels**
- Use Solo to isolate each track
- Set target peak of -6dB per track
- Use compression to control dynamics
